Romantasy is a relatively new term for a genre focused on a blend of romance and fantasy. Its popularity has spiked in recent years, drawing in readers who crave both emotional depth and imaginative worldbuilding. Even so, the genre is often dismissed as low-quality or “slop” due to its heavy reliance on tropes, popularity on social media, and young female audience. However, this perception overlooks the complexity, creativity, and cultural significance that many romantasy works offer. Beneath the surface of popular tropes and viral trends lies a diverse body of storytelling that explores power, identity, love, and morality.

This website exists to challenge those assumptions. Its purpose is to highlight the depth and variety within romantasy, bringing attention to works that demonstrate strong writing, intricate plots, and compelling character development. While the genre certainly includes lighter, more accessible reads, it also contains stories that rival traditional fantasy in scope and ambition. Dismissing the entire category ignores the voices of talented authors and the experiences of readers who find value in these narratives.

By creating this space, I aim to provide thoughtful discussion, recommendations, and analysis that take romantasy seriously as a literary form. Of course, not every romantasy novel is a work of genius. I have my gripes with many of them, even many of the most popular ones (looking at you, ACOTAR). However, rather than reducing the genre to stereotypes, I aim to explore what makes it resonate so strongly with its audience and maybe convince a skeptical reader or two why romantasy deserves far more respect than it is often given.

Covers

Book covers are usually the first point of contact between a reader and a story. Romantasy covers in particular walk a difficult line, having to visually represent the blend of its parent genres. Generally, romantasy covers tend to err on the side of fantasy: elements like glowing magic, weapons, or symbolic (and often magically charged) items are mainstays of the genre’s covers.

Covers also reflect how the genre is marketed and perceived. Romantasy is frequently dismissed as overly indulgent or unserious, particularly because of its association with female readership. As such, by leaning more towards the fantastic rather than the romantic, romantasy can appeal to wider audiences.

Protagonists

Protagonists are the vessels through which we experience the story. As such, a good protagonist can often make or break a story. Because the genre is heavily marketed towards and enjoyed by women, the vast majority of romantasy protagonists are women. The genre also draws a lot of criticism from readers and reviewers for being very white or cagey about race. Most romantasy leads and side characters are white, with diverse casts being more the result of collective fandom headcanons than any particular effort on the behalf of authors. As such, I’ve done my best to highlight several novels with more diverse casts and protagonists.
