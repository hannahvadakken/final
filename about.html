About Us

Romantic reads are a community dedicated to the addicting combination of high-stakes magic and heart-pounding desire: Romantasy. Whether you’re here for the "Enemies to Lovers" tension, the "Touch Her and You Die" protective energy, or the intricate "Soulmate Bonds" that defy destiny, you’ve found your place.

Our mission is to help you navigate the ever-expanding realms of fae courts, fallen kingdoms, and forbidden magic. We dive deep into the tropes that make our hearts race, providing nuanced reviews that aim to separate the must-reads from the skips. From spicy underground hits to the latest viral sensations, we analyze the world-building and chemistry that define the genre.

If you believe that a dragon is a girl’s best friend and a slow-burn romance is worth the wait, grab a book off the top of your TBR. Let's lose ourselves in the next great adventure, one page at a time.

Meet The Team!

Editor-in-Chief: Bartholomew Higgins

Bartholomew is a minimalist by design but a maximalist by temperament. Consisting of exactly four straight lines and a slightly lopsided circle for a head, he represents the pinnacle of low-fidelity aesthetic. Despite lacking a heart (anatomically speaking), Bartholomew is the team’s foremost expert on Regency-era "slow burn" romances. He spent three years attempting to flip the pages of Pride and Prejudice with his rigid, unbending arms, eventually succeeding through sheer structural defiance and a yearning for Mr. Darcy.

"If there isn't a misunderstanding in Chapter 2 that could be solved by a thirty-second conversation, I simply don't want it. - Bartholomew"

Fantasy Analyst: Sir Scribblesworth III

Sir Scribblesworth III is the only member of the team drawn with a "shaky hand," giving him a permanent aura of existential vibration. This erratic linework is often mistaken for caffeine jitters, but it is actually the physical manifestation of his passion for hard magic systems and 800-page dragon epics.

Life is fleeting, much like a charcoal sketch in a rainstorm. A well-indexed appendix of ancient dragon dialects is forever. - Sir Scribblesworth III

Social Media Manager: Dorothy "Dotty" Lineson

Dotty is unique among her luscious locks, which she twirls endlessly around her fingers while daydreaming about "He falls first" scenarios. Dotty’s passion lies at the intersection of spicy romance and high-stakes fantasy. She often argues that a well-constructed magic system has more load-bearing integrity than a suspension bridge, especially when a brooding shadow-wielder is involved.

"People ask how I handle the 'wings and fangs' trope without any internal organs. It’s simple: I don't need a pulse to recognize that a 500-year-old Fae King is actually just a misunderstood softie who needs a library and a hug." - Dotty
